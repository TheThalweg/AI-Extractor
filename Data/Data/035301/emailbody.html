Key takeaways
  • This is consistent with the broader industry trend, including the price hikes for AliCloud and Baidu Cloud.
  • This also echoes management's comments during the 4Q25 earnings call that Tencent Cloud now enjoys a better pricing environment...
  • ...with external cloud services revenue expected to grow robustly in 2026 while sustaining profitability.
  • We also see a positive read-across for Alibaba.

Details: Tencent Cloud announced today that it will raise list prices 5%, effective May 9, 2026, mainly on:

  • Products and services related to AI computing power;
  • TKE (Tencent Kubernetes Engine) native-node container products and services; and
  • Products and services related to Elastic MapReduce (EMR).

The new prices will apply at next renewal or new purchase and will not affect products and services purchased before May 9, 2026.

The company cited surging global AI computing demand and higher hardware supply chain costs.

More on the positive read-across: Tencentï¿½s move supports the idea that pricing power in China's AI/cloud infrastructure is improving, which should help industry monetization and margin discipline. That is supportive for AliCloud, which has already announced price increases for select AI computing and storage services effective April 18.
