Asia-Pacific Weekly Kickstart: MXAPJ rebounded 7% on a risk‑on rally driven by a conditional US‑Iran ceasefire, alongside stronger Asian FX and a return of foreign inflows; Korea and Taiwan led gains (both +10%)
11 April 2026 | 2:10AM SGT | Research | Portfolio Strategy

MXAPJ rose 7.2%, with Korea and Taiwan (both +10%), India and Indonesia (each +6%) outperforming Malaysia (flat), Singapore (+1%), and the Philippines (+2%). Tech Hardware & Semis, Capital Goods, and Materials led gains, while Energy, Health Care, and Telecom Services lagged. EM Asia saw foreign inflows of US$7.8bn, led by Taiwan (+US$6.1bn) and Korea (+US$3.5bn), while India saw outflows (-US$1.8bn). Iran and the US agreed to a conditional two‑week ceasefire, during which shipping traffic is meant to be allowed through the Strait of Hormuz. Our commodity analyst lowered the near‑term oil price forecast but kept 2026Q3/Q4 forecasts unchanged. In the US, the FOMC released its latest meeting minutes, noting that “almost all” participants see the Committee as “well positioned” at the current policy rate. The ISM services index declined, while new orders for durable goods fell in February as expected. Real GDP growth was revised down in 2025Q4 due to a downward revision to inventory accumulation. In Asia, China inflation turned positive last month for the first time since late 2022, while Taiwan inflation eased. Hong Kong PMI fell in March. Korea and India kept policy rates unchanged; India services PMI remained robust on strong export orders. Upcoming key data/events are summarized within.
 

Charts of the Week

Oil Shock Pulse Check: While our commodities analysts nudged down the 2026Q2 Brent forecast to US$90/bbl following a two‑week ceasefire, they continue to see risks to oil prices skewed to the upside on net, reflecting the potential for longer‑lasting supply disruptions and persistent crude production losses. The Geopolitical Risk (GPR) index has pulled back from its recent peak but remains elevated amid a persistently high geopolitical‑risk backdrop. MXAPJ has rebounded 11% from its trough and is down only 4% since March, dragged down by Indonesia, Philippines, and Korea, while Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Australia have held up better.

Mutual Fund Positioning: Based on preliminary EPFR data for March (~25% of total AUM reported), Asian mutual funds increased exposure to Korea, while trimming allocations to China and Taiwan versus benchmark. Sector‑wise, China Industrials saw the largest allocation increase, while China Banks, Tech, and Autos experienced the most pronounced cuts.
