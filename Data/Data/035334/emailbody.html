Richard Privorotsky ï¿½ EMEA head 1D trading:

 

Let's Talk: Ceasefire talks meant to commence today (at least I think they are...some debate if Iranian delegation is actually there). Think clear both sides have a huge distance to cover but what gives me some optimism is that weï¿½re getting snippets from the press around Chinaï¿½s involvement "Iranian officials reportedly credited a last-minute push by China with securing their acceptance, a claim soon after validated by Trump" (BBG). That matters because China is really the only major power with the leverage to bridge an outcome on both sides...if they can act as a security guarantor and sit at the center of this, you can start to feel more constructive on the eventual deal. Thatï¿½s not to say Iran would suddenly lose its leverage in the middle of a negotiationï¿½ the reported cap of ~15 ships per day implies a real supply constraint, think oil stays bid until you've got a signed deal on the table.  Net netï¿½ first rounds probably go nowhere, positions are far apart, but as long as China stays engaged I lean marginally more optimisticï¿½ though with low conviction (I have taken note that US military hardware has only continued to build despite the talks).

 

Vol Compression/Technicals: Vol feels like itï¿½s cutting tails. Youï¿½ve got a window of calm (2 week ceasefire)ï¿½ VIX back sub-20, mechanical compression driving re-leveraging, spot feeding CTA flowsï¿½ a bit of a self-reinforcing loop. Positioning still a tailwindï¿½ ï¿½US-listed ETF shorts fell -7.4% yesterday, the largest 1-day covering since Aug ï¿½20 and the 3rd largest in the past decade, led by large-cap equity, then credit and small capsï¿½ (h/t John Flood). It really does look like a macro cover bid more than anything fundamental.

 

On softwareï¿½ worth paying attention as IGV is back at the lows. To my comments on Mythos yday its clear the same concern is  resurfacing on terminal valuations and hence multiples in the software space. The major winner is Momentum...AI winners on the hardware / enabler side keep acceleratingï¿½ TSMC March revenues up ~45% YoY, all-time highsï¿½ ï¿½weï¿½re gonna need a bigger boat.ï¿½ Meanwhile, corporates on the other side ï¿½need a bigger moat.ï¿½ I donï¿½t think you can just dismiss the derating in software as noiseï¿½ the implication is bigger. Advanced compute is an affront to large parts of knowledge workï¿½ that challenges terminal multiples across a huge swathe of the market. My AI view hasnï¿½t changedï¿½ itï¿½s boosting EPS via capex into physical assets, but compressing multiples because terminal value is massively uncertain. Concentrated pool of winners and larger impact to multiples.

 

On inflationï¿½ Chinaï¿½s factory-gate prices turning positive for the first time in years matters at the margin. Feels like goods inflation was already picking up before the energy shock. Labor is still oddï¿½ a ï¿½not hiring / not firingï¿½ market, but wages drifting higher for job switchers. Net net thatï¿½s still inflationary. The Fed  at best probably has its hands tied. CPI  may not tell you much immediately given energy timing, but the key question is what the underlying baseline looked like pre-shock. (see below)

 

On riskï¿½ flows are supportive. Vol compression + CTA + still a lack of real longs means dips likely get bought. But weï¿½re also back into a long gamma regime in S&P 500 that suppresses movement. I lean towards selling upsideï¿½ I just donï¿½t see much of it. Between software derating, rates, and what still looks like a capped credit market, the risk/reward feels poor. Outside of AI hardware, Iï¿½m not sure what youï¿½re chasingï¿½ maybe that is the whole game. Comfortable holding longs in those pockets, but broader equities still feel like theyï¿½ll ultimately follow ratesï¿½ and those are already starting to lag. Watching CPI.

1) FLOWS RECOVERING:  
 Activity waned this week but that was largely dependent on the Easter Holidays which were around the region. Hong Kong for example was on holiday for the first two days of the week. 2) CHINA REBOUNDS: 

A-share saw a relief-style rebound this week as global risk sentiment improved after the temporary US-Iran ceasefire. SHCOMP +2.22%/ChiNext +5.51% wtd (as of Friday Morning). For onshore investors, the risk appetite saw meaningful improvement with market turnover went from 1.6 trillion level, lowest since Dec 2025, to 2.4 trillion RMB after the announcement. From a sector rotation angle, TMT sharply increased its turnover contribution (chart below) and with TMTï¿½s t/over contribution rising to levels higher than before the Middle East conflict started. Locals-favored AI related themes, such as CPO and Memory names, saw sharp rebound to drive the indices higher. On the other hand, what's also notable is the healthcare sector, which outperformed in previous weekï¿½s but have cooled down with investors are chasing tech related growth names. Utility names that were favored at the start of the conflict driven by HALO plays also saw some rotation out of the space. Though the conviction on the macro side remains mixed, the rebound and sharper rise in TMT turnover suggest local institutions and retail is quick to re-engage when headline risk eases. Looking at total buying on margin as % of A share total turnover, the number rose toward 10% and reached its highest level since the Middle East conflict began. However, it hasn't returned to the peaks this year seen in early Jan, suggesting retail sentiment has improved but is not yet back in full risk-on territory. Looking ahead, all eyes will be on US-Iran talks this weekend and we expect this continue to be a key swing factor, and a more concrete de-escalation will likely further improve risk appetite and create room for a broader recovery. ï¿½ Ty Leon Ma

3) KOREA REBOUNDS: 

KOSPI rallied +7.5% WTD as bids were broadly back in cyclicals after the two-week ceasefire headlines (KRXSEMI +9.5%, KRXAUTO +4.4%, GSXAKHOC +10.7%, GSXAKPOW +8.9%, GSXAKREV +7.3%, KRXBANK +5.1% WTD), while the sentiment still appears fragile (i.e. turnover popped on Wednesday +51% vs. 20D avg, then faded yesterday) with focus remaining on geopolitical developments (i.e. US/Iran talks) as ceasefire is cautiously holding up. Foreigners turned as net buyers this week (+$2.6bn) to snap its 7-week net-selling streak, driven by its record daily net-buying in Hynix on April 8th (+$850mn). Local instos jointly net bought with FIIs (+$442mn), with buying focused in Tech (+$170mn) and Financials (+$150mn), while retail investors were heavy profit-takers (-$4.4bn). This weekï¿½s focus was in memory as SEC(005930) +9.6% WTD and SK HYNIX(000660) +13.9% WTD rallied amid sharp inflow from FIIs (+$1.6bn in SEC and +$570mn in SK HYNIX), thanks to the improving geopolitical developments coupled with SECï¿½s robust 1Q Prelims (OP @ W52.7tn, exceeding the bullish estimate of W54tn and even FY25 annual OP of W43.6tn). Our pad was 2.8x better Buyers this week, with a sharp increase in activity levels from Wednesday. Buying was focused around memory (in both Hynix and SEC), Defense (dip-buyers emerged with GSXAKDEF +2.5% underperformance vs. index), and Telcos. Lastly, it is worth flagging that despite the recent volatility in March, the earnings upgrade cycle for Kospi continued. Valuation compressed significantly with KOSPI forward P/E falling to 7.58x as of end-March, or approximately -2.0 standard deviations below its historical average. This represents the lowest level since the Global Financial Crisis (6.3x), with other notable troughs observed during the European debt crisis in 2011 (7.6x) and the first US-China trade war in 2018 (7.7x).ï¿½ Ty Emily Jee / Chris Cha

4) TAIWAN REBOUNDS:  
Taiwan returned with big rally with index up over 7% WTD and TAIEX close to 35k points. Foreigners gradually turned risk-on with over US$4.3bn inflows WTD (US$4bn on Wednesday alone). 5) INDIA REBOUNBS:  
 India saw an extension of gains this week as Nifty (+4.7% WTD) ended in green for five straight sessions before seeing a partial reversal yesterday as it faces some resistance at the 24k level. The index showed strength on Mon & Tue despite the elevated oil prices, while the US-Iran ceasefire announcement helped bring the major move on Wed, inline with other peers, as Nifty closed with gains of +3.8%. The trend reversal has been broad based as the Midcap and Smallcap indices (+5.9% & +6.2% WTD) have consistently outperformed Nifty. Investors also reacted to multiple business updates from corporates (Jewellers & Consumer names stood out), while the Q4 earnings season kicked off yesterday with TCS (no major surprise).
