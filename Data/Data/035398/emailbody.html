  • The playbook worked again. If the bottom last Monday continues to hold, the equity market would have again stuck close to the script around past geopolitical shocks, of sharp but surprisingly short-lived selloffs and V-shaped rebounds (Of Politics And Pullbacks, Sep 2017). The S&P 500 is now back into the tight range that prevailed four months going into the war.
  • Our measure of equity positioning has begun to move up but is still underweight (-0.33sd, 27th percentile). As we highlighted two weeks ago, on a de-escalation in the conflict, we expect it to rise to neutral with room for both systematic strategies and discretionary investors to raise exposures, taking the S&P 500 close to 6900, but hit a near-term ceiling there as the focus shifts back to other concerns around AI bubbles and disruptions, credit losses, and growth (Reflecting War, AI and Growth Gloom, Mar 20 2026).
  • Q1 earnings season beginning next week should show exceptionally strong growth and modest beats. The beginning of the Q1 earnings season next week is timely. As we pointed out in our preview earlier this week, we see exceptionally strong growth (19%) but modest beats (2.6%). The broadening of growth that began in Q3 has continued and we forecast 10 sectors will post positive growth this earnings season, compared to just 2 in Q2 last year. However, we would reiterate that the broadening in earnings growth has not come at the expense of the former leaders, and we see MCG & Tech (36%) and Financials (20%) leading again.
  • Will it be enough? Underlying concerns may not be resolved with Q1 earnings growth but whatï¿½s priced in has already shifted dramatically. Despite continued strong earnings growth the S&P 500 is at the same level as it was in late October, reflecting the near-perfect rotation out of MCG & Tech and into most other sectors. But the rotation in our reading is now done. In late October, that is, during the Q3 earnings season when a broadening in earnings growth sparked the rotation, the performance of MCG & Tech relative to everything else was at the top of its decade-long trend channel. It is now at bottom and has been hovering there for 2 months (Tech Bounces Off The Bottom Of The Channel, Feb 6 2026). Positioning in equities is starkly disconnected from earnings growth, in line with an imminent drop, especially for MCG & Tech and the Financials.
