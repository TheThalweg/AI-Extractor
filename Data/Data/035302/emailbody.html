In This Edition

United States: Interest Rates

Back from the brink

The ceasefire has helped the front-end. The market's modal view should be for easing, given favorable inflation trends and stable long-term inflation expectations despite higher oil, though labor data suggest paring back the likelihood of aggressive cuts. Fiscal policy poses risk to the long end.

10 min read

United States: Treasuries

Treasury auctions: Ides of March

March auctions tailed up to the 10y point, a rare occurrence in the Treasury market. Just-released auction investor category data show that foreign investors stepped back, while investment funds at the long-end remained resilient. As rate vol subsided, April auctions have fared better so far.

4 min read

United States: Inflation Markets

Awkward optics

We discuss why forwards project 1y BEs to drop to 1% by the end of June, and why the upcoming strong carry period reinforces our recommendation to be long 5y TIPS.

5 min read

Derivatives Strategy

Belly spreads have more upside than front-end spreads

While uncertainty about the Middle East conflict persists, spreads appear to be finding support. We maintain a preference for belly spreads and recommend beta-weighted 2s7s spread curve steepeners. The trade will retain upside if spreads recover while mitigating risk from fiscal-driven tightening.

4 min read

Euro Area: Rates Strategy

Uneasy

The ceasefire has brought some relief to EUR rates but the set-up feels uneasy. A sustained decline in oil price is a necessary condition for further removal of rate hike pricing but some residual hike premium is likely to remain. We also explore why Bund ASWs have stalled after the great fall.

7 min read

EUR and UK: Inflation Markets

Two steps forward, one step back

The Middle East ceasefire may support tactical re-steepening of the EUR inflation curve, but we expect the bulk of inflation risk premium to remain concentrated in the sub-5y area. We continue to recommend 5y5y USCPI-HICPx wideners.

3 min read

UK: Rates Strategy

The reluctant fundamentalists (redux)

Market pricing has the first full hike by July. But the inflationary outlook for 2026 is materially worse and risk of an early precautionary hike at either April or June's MPC meeting is underpriced.

4 min read
