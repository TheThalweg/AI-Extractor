EM Weekly Kickstart: MSCI EM rebounded 7% w/w following the US-Iran ceasefire; Tech-heavy North Asia led, Saudi Arabia underperformed; FII buying resumed after weeks of outflows
10 April 2026 | 5:17PM BST | Research | Portfolio Strategy

●

Performance: MXEF rebounded 7% w/w as energy prices moderated on the US-Iran ceasefire. Tech-heavy North Asia (+10%) and India (+7%) led among majors, while Saudi Arabia (+1%) underperformed amid lower oil.

●

Earnings/valuations: MXEF is trading at 11.6x NTM P/E (-0.7 s.d, 10-yr avg). EM 2026E EPS was revised up 5.3% over the past month led by Korea and Brazil.

●

Flows: EM equities saw buying of US$7.5bn w/w led by Taiwan (+US$6.1bn) and Korea (+US$3.5bn), while Brazil saw outflows (-US$0.2bn).

●

Macro: Central banks were on hold across Korea, India, Poland, Peru. Inflation prints surprised to the upside in Philippines, Colombia, Egypt, Chile, and to the downside in Turkiye, Czechia, Hungary, Taiwan, Mexico.
 

Focus: Tensions on Pause

●

Following the announcement of the US-Iran ceasefire, MSCI EM has gained ~5%, mostly in line with DM equities. LatAm, North Asia and EM Europe have led the rebound with markets that lagged most since the start of the conflict -- South Africa, UAE, Korea, Egypt -- seeing the strongest rebounds. On the other hand, Saudi Arabia and Colombia -- which had gained during the conflict -- have continued to trade defensively as oil prices remain elevated on continued uncertainty.

●

MSCI EM has retraced only half of the gains from the trough, in line with DM ex-US peers, leaving room for upside if the situation stabilizes further. LatAm and EM Europe have recouped losses from respective troughs, while China and ASEAN have retraced the least. Foreign selling has paused with US$9bn in inflows following US$79bn in selling since the start of the conflict. Overall, Korea/Taiwan have seen the highest outflows, while Brazil has seen net inflows over the conflict period.

●

Overall, we continue to believe that valuation and positioning have reset for EM equities, providing attractive potential index returns if deescalation of the conflict persists. We remain OW Korea, China, Brazil and South Africa among larger EM markets. Brazil stands out as a relative beneficiary, given its energy sector gains, and potential for a recovery in domestic pockets that have lagged. We view North Asia as better placed than South Asia. Also, the sharp re-pricing in South African equities presents attractive risk reward.
