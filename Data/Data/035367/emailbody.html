EGB spreads entered the Iran war at very rich valuations vs other asset classes, especially due to their relative resilience to the AI-related prior risk-off move as well as the reduction in OATs' political risk premia. This, in conjunction with Europe's dependence on energy imports and the rise in absolute yields led to the EGB spreads' underperformance since the beginning of March vs other risky assets such as credit spreads. The unwinding of crowded positions such as long front-end EUR and long EGB carry trades also contributed to this underperformance.

Among EGBs, it was BTPs that cheapened the most - of course not only in absolute terms - but in beta adjusted terms too. The fact that Italy is more exposed to the energy shock and that after a long period of calmness the domestic political situation generates headlines, also contributed to this idiosyncratic cheapening of BTPs vs their peers. Hence we don't think this is unjustified and have not leaned against it. In our 2026 outlook we had identified a broader risk-off move as the most likely trigger for a re-widening in EGB spreads.
