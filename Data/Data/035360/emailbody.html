Japan Weekly Kickstart: Geopolitical uncertainties remain, but Japan appears to be in a relatively strong position
10 April 2026 | 10:10PM JST | Research | Portfolio Strategy
 

Summary of the week

TOPIX: 3,739.85 (2.6%) / NK225: 56,924.11 (7.2%)

●

Top sectors: Non-Ferrous Metals, Glass & Ceramics

●

Bottom sectors: Mining, Oil & Coal

In this week's Japan Weekly Kickstart we discuss the market impact of the current pause in the war in Iran. Although this week's ceasefire agreement has been subsequently described as 'a fragile truce', TSE flow data suggests that foreigners had turned net buyers of Japanese equities from the start of this month. According to our Japan Econ team (LINK), Japan does appear to be in a somewhat stronger position versus other countries given its easier access to alternative crude oil and LNG supply routes, as well as its comparatively large amount of strategic oil reserves. After TPX and NKY corrected -11% and -13% respectively in March, they have subsequently rebounded +7% and +11% from their lows so far in April. TPX is now only +5% away from its February all-time-high, and the Japanese market will be entering a seasonally-strong period for Corporate Governance-related catalysts from the end of April, driven by earnings announcements, shareholder returns policies, MTPs and AGMs.

●

YTD-winners back in focus so far this month as geopolitical concerns moderate: As can be seen in Exhibit 6 below, there has been a sharp reversal in the performance of many of our thematic and sub-sector indices in April versus March. So far this month, AI-related indices such as SPE (+22% mtd) and AI Beneficiaries (+19% mtd) have been amongst this month's best performers, but were amongst the worst-performers during March (both were -18% mom).

●

Draft Corporate Governance Code released: The FSA released an updated draft of its Corporate Governance Code last week (LINK). It suggests an increased focus on factors such as capital efficiency, cash allocations and cross-shareholding unwinds.

●

Foreigners and Domestics were net buyers, Individuals were net sellers: According to the latest data from the TSE, for the week Mar 30 – Apr 3, Foreigners and Domestic Institutions net bought ¥1.9tn and ¥139bn of TSE Prime cash equities respectively, while Individuals net sold -¥450bn.
