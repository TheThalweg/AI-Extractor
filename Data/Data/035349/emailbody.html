 The twisted path towards an exit

The ceasefire with Iran has provided President Trump with a welcome off-ramp. Many details of the agreement remain unclear or even disputed. What is known so far suggests that it is more of a framework for negotiations than a fully defined agreement. The situation remains fragile and improvised, and far from a true resolution. Therefore, the set of potential outcomes remains wide, with probabilities quite evenly distributed. Yet we believe that some form of agreement, formal or tacit, is the most likely outcome. In such a setting, the oil price should settle at somewhat lower levels than today, but not fall to pre-war levels. While not an optimal outcome, it would limit the economic fallout compared to a prolonged conflict. We provide a Q&A on the war in Iran, focussing on key questions regarding geopolitical consequences, the macroeconomic impact and implications for financial markets.

 

Finally, we take a look at the Q1 reporting season in the US. Consensus expectations project another quarter with 13% EPS growth despite the war in Iran. Financials should benefit from higher rates, elevated volatility and strong M&A activity in Q1. Tech consensus earnings have been upgraded strongly into the season, but should be broadly met. One reason for concern is slowing hyperscaler earnings growth, which may eventually impact capex spending plans.
