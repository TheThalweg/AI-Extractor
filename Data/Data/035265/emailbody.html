  • Last month's market activity was largely shaped by the ongoing conflict in the Middle East and the roll on March 20th (March 27th for CDX HY). On roll day, Main S44 closed +1bp and S45 closed +6bps, suggesting the roll was worth around 5bps. In XO, those moves were S44 +7bps and S45 +39bps, suggesting the roll impact was around 32bps. In the $ space, CDX IG S45/S46 suggested a 5bps roll, and CDX HY S45/S46 indicated a 20bps roll.
  • Beyond the roll, as the US-Iran conflict escalated through the month Main and XO were driven to local wides of 74bps and 363bps respectively by March 27th. Global rates also sold off amidst concerns that oil and gas prices would become anchored at higher levels, causing a surge in financing costs that kept primary volumes subdued (see our ï¿½-Credit Supply Monitor) and helped cash outperform vs. synthetics. However, sentiment began to improve this week with positive signals regarding a potential conflict resolution triggering a multi-asset rally yesterday. This resulted in a partial reversal of synthetic underperformance vs. cash, with Main, XO, ï¿½IG, and ï¿½HY closing 2bps, 9bps, 1bp, and 4bps tighter respectively on March 31st.
  • Since the conflict began on Feb 27th ï¿½IG spreads are up +15bps/+10bps and ï¿½HY spreads are +47bps/+32bps (G-/ASW-spread) while Main and XO have widened +16bps and +93bps, partly due to the roll. Despite this week's synthetic outperformance, Main's spread pick up vs. ï¿½IG G-spread remains historically high while its pick up vs. ï¿½IG ASW-spread is at the highs since Aug'23. Similarly, even though the pick up of XO vs. ï¿½HY has retraced slightly, it's still near the highs since Aug'25 (G-spread) and March'25 (ASW-spread). It's a similar story in $ where CDX IG-$IG is near multi-year highs and CDX HY-$HY is near the highs since Nov'23.
  • March also highlighted a significant outperformance of $ vs. ï¿½, underscoring Europe's greater exposure to an energy-price shock. This contrasts the moves mentioned in our previous publication when fears around AI disruption and the link to US private credit had driven decompression in the CDX HY/CDX IG ratio as XO/Main compressed in February.
