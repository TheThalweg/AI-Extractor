  • Chart of the week: The Monthly Labor Survey revealed that scheduled cash earnings increased more than 3%, suggesting strongly that wages are on the rise. This level is consistent with the BoJ's 2% inflation target and will encourage its move toward monetary policy normalization.
  • Indicators over the next two weeks: The focus in customs trade statistics (22 April) will be the extent of the decline in oil imports due to the Middle East situation, while the key point in the PMI (23 April) will be the deterioration in corporate sentiment. In the March nationwide CPI (24 April), we believe the upswing in gasoline prices will be offset by slowing growth in food prices.
  • Monetary policy: Governor Kazuo Ueda is unlikely to offer hints at future monetary policy in his address to the Trust Companies Association of Japan on 13 April. More important will be the 2026 Spring Meetings of the IMF and World Bank starting the same day. Discussions on the global economy and the results of the latest World Economic Outlook could influence the BoJ's thinking at its meeting later this month.
