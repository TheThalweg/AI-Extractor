Changes to flag this week:

●

Lowered our 3/6m Brent crude oil price forecasts to $84/$80/bbl (from $86/$81/bbl) to reflect reduced near-term risk premium and rising oil flows through the Strait of Hormuz amid the 2-week Iran conflict ceasefire.

●

Lowered our 3/12m European natural gas (TTF) price forecasts to 44/29 EUR/MWh (from 51/33 EUR/MWh) to reflect particularly low China LNG demand.

●

Lowered our 3/6/12m LME copper price forecasts to $12,635/$12,535/$12,265/mt (from $13,000/$12,800/$12,400/mt) to reflect softer demand amid the slowdown in global growth.
 

Watching

●

WATCH THE IRAN WAR. While the US and Iran have agreed to a 2-week ceasefire, the situation remains very fluid and we continue to see risks to energy prices as skewed to the upside from potentially longer supply disruptions which could have significant impacts on economies and markets.

●

Globally, we expect real GDP growth of 2.4% yoy in 2026 amid growing headwinds from higher energy prices owing to the Iran war. We expect global core inflation to decline to 2.3% by the end of 2026, reflecting a fading tariff boost and further normalization in shelter and wage inflation but a boost from higher energy prices.

●

In the US, we expect real GDP growth of 2.0% on a Q4/Q4 basis in 2026, reflecting a drag from higher oil prices, tighter financial conditions, and elevated geopolitical risk. We see a 30% probability of entering a recession over the next 12 months given upside risk to oil prices. We expect core PCE inflation to fall to 2.5% yoy by the end of 2026, as the tariff boost fades and wage and shelter inflation continue to cool, though higher oil prices provide some offset. We expect the unemployment rate to end 2026 at 4.6%.

●

We expect the Fed to deliver two 25bp cuts this year in September and December for a terminal rate range of 3-3.25%, although the risk that the Fed could remain on hold all year has risen somewhat.

●

In the Euro area, we expect below-potential real GDP growth of 0.6% on a Q4/Q4 basis in 2026, reflecting higher energy prices and tighter financial conditions owing to the Iran conflict. We expect core inflation to rise to a peak of 2.5% yoy in 3Q26 and end the year at 2.4% yoy amid higher energy prices.

●

We expect the ECB to deliver two 25bp hikes this year in April and June to a peak policy rate of 2.5% before cutting back to our 2% estimate of the nominal neutral rate in 2027 as inflation declines and the economy weakens.

●

In China, we expect real GDP growth of 4.7% yoy in 2026 as resilient export growth, continued government policy easing, and a decreasing drag from the ongoing property market downturn offset headwinds from higher energy prices and sluggish domestic demand. We expect CPI/PPI inflation to rise to 1.0%/1.0% yoy this year owing to higher global commodity prices, government policy efforts, and a low base.
