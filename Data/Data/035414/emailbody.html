Weekly Gaming & Lodging Data Recap

In this note, we aim to provide a summary of the most recent Gaming & Lodging data for the week and implications for our covered companies, plus some one-off analysis of the recent trends and market implications. For this edition, we cover; 1) weekly / 1Q26 RevPAR trends, our estimates for where 1Q26 Lodging-Corps RevPAR is tracking, the RevPAR two-year stack and implications for the remainder of the year, and thoughts on the Economy RevPAR acceleration, 2) reviewing the March OSB data to date and where digital operator GGR is tracking Y/Y for the 1Q26 to date, and 3) reviewing the March regional GGR and where the data is tracking versus our operator GGR forecasts.

Portfolio Managers Summary:
  • RevPAR Trends: U.S. RevPAR continues to be strong overall, excluding calendar shifts, despite elevated gas price and geopolitical risks. Over the last four weeks, U.S. RevPAR is up 3.5% Y/Y, as ADR is up 2.8% Y/Y and Occupancy is up 70 bps Y/Y.
  • Lodging C-Corps: We estimate Lodging C-Corps 1Q26 systemwide RevPAR continues to track ahead of consensus forecasts for HLT, MAR, H, WH, and CHH.
  • RevPAR Outlook: We estimate the 1Q26 U.S. Industry RevPAR two-year stack is up ~5.9%. If we keep the two-year stack constant for the remaining three quarters of the year, it would imply year over year U.S. Industry RevPAR growth for the 2Q26 of ~+6.4% Y/Y, 3Q26 ~+7.4% Y/Y, and 4Q26 ~+7.0% Y/Y. We believe this would create upside risk to 2026 RevPAR outlooks, should trends remain constant, combined with our estimate for the 1Q26 RevPAR tracking above consensus forecasts.
  • OSB Handle: Handle continues to be soft.For March, we estimate same-store handle, for states that have reported, is down ~2.8% Y/Y, compared to February down ~2.1% Y/Y, and January down ~3.5% Y/Y. March handle by state thus far includes; NY -4.7% Y/Y, WV -19.1%, IN -6.5%, IA -6.8%, MD +2.8%, KS -6.5%, and NC +6.0%. Note, NY handle for the week ending 4/5 was down 9.1% Y/Y and WV handle for the week ending 4/4 was down 31.1% Y/Y.
  • Regional GGR: We believe March Brick & Mortar GGR, for states that have reported thus far, has decelerated compared to February and January GGR. On a same-store calendar adjusted basis, we estimate March GGR is up ~60 bps Y/Y, compared to February up ~3.8% Y/Y, and January up ~1.5% Y/Y.
  • Regional GGR by Operator: For the regional gaming operators under our coverage; BYD MW&S GGR is tracking ~1.4% above our forecast, CZR Regional GGR is tracking ~1.6% below our forecast, MGM Regional GGR is tracking ~10 bps above our forecast, and PENN Regional GGR is tracking ~ 50 bps above our forecast.

For our full review and analysis of the data please see the body of this note. Our databases are available upon request.
