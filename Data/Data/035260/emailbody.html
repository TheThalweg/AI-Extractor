Busy Ming {Ticker: 1768.HK, Closing Price: 352.20 HKD, Target Price: 516.00 HKD, Recommendation: Buy}

Click here for the Model

Busy Ming hosted a results briefing on April 1, following the release of its in-line 2025 results. We expect the market to react positively for: 1) the company's robust store expansion outlook, and 2) management's clear articulation of strategies to enhance store and SKU quality and operational efficiency amid industry competition.

Takeaways from the analyst briefing
  • Management remains positive on the company's long-term store expansion, along with the snack & beverage specialty retail industry to grow to around 100,000 total stores. This could be driven by consumer traffic potentials. Specifically for 2026, the company guided for roughly 5,000 net new stores.
  • They also highlighted that quality of new stores will be supported by optimized site selection leveraging digital tools and enhanced franchisee empowerment with standardized store operation protocols. Management emphasized that a strong unit economics (UE) model lays the foundation for successful store expansion and fosters solidified relationship with franchisees.
  • Busy Ming saw same store sales YoY improved in the 2026 Chinese New Year period, and they expected 2026 same-store GMV to improve YoY given: 1) More effective product replenishment and display strategies; 2) Exploration into new categories, including hot food (e.g., grilled sausage/egg tarts) and refrigerated/frozen snacks; 3) Membership growth, with registered members reaching ~210mn by 2025 (vs 120mn in 2024). Management highlighted that the ticket average from their members is 70-80% higher than that of non-members.
  • Busy Ming is confident in its ability to navigate competition against traditional retailers: 80% of their SKUs possess differentiated features that resonate with consumersï¿½ pursuit of joy. The company did not plan to expand the private-label business, which might dilute its SKU's differentiation feature and involve the company in intensified competition with traditional retailers.
  • Meanwhile, the company is committed to improving operational efficiency by supply chain optimization, standardized store operations, and digitalization efforts. This, together with operating leverage, should help keep OPEX under control and support long-term margin improvement.
Valuation: PT lifted to HKD516.00; maintain Buy

Factoring in Busy Ming's 2025 results and the higher-than-expected store opening potentials, we raise our 2026-27E adjusted net profit by 6% on average. Our DCF-based PT is, therefore, lifted to HKD516.00 from HKD488.00, implying 25x 2026E P/E (based on 2026E adjusted net profit of close to RMB4.0bn).
