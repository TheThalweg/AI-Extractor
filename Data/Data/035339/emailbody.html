Internet & Media (I&M) underperforms Software & Servicesï¿½ 2026 remains a stock pickers play. There has been a lot of discussion on the significant compression seen in Software & Services, yet over the last 12M, it has actually outperformed the Internet & Media sector using the median of the stocks (i.e. not market cap weighted). Clearly, there is a spread of performance in Software & Services, but we still find it relatively interesting to see it, in aggregate, outperform the majority of the Internet & Media sub-sectors (Classifieds, eCommerce, Food delivery) with only the Media & Entertainment sub-sector trading in-line (and 'only' -10% LTM). Clear sector laggards in the last 12M are Hemnet -70%, Jahez -62%, Ubisoft -55%, WPP 54%, HelloFresh -47%, Rightmove -42%. with Saudi-based Rasan the clear sector outperformer +73%.

The AI debate for Classifieds is similar to Software & Services. There has also been a wide range of debate on the sell-off seen in Classifieds, which, similar to Software & Services, has been attached to the potential disruption risk from AI (somewhat unfairly at times, in our view). The median P/E multiple for Software & Services has compressed 19% in the last 12M, with much of that coming year-to-date (-15%) to 16x P/E. Classifieds still trade at a slightly higher multiple at 17x P/E, but with the compression far more pronounced at -42% LTM and also -20% YTD. Clearly, this is partly attributable to the high 'starting point' from last year (28x P/E) and leaves it trading still at a premium to the wider sector.

The overall I&M sector trades, on aggregate, at 13x P/E (Classifieds 17x, eCommerce 13x, Media & Entertainment 11x, EMEA Food Delivery 14x), we see relative opportunities across the sector. 2026 is a stock pickers play. Our top picks are UMG and Publicis (Media & Entertainment), Scout24 (Classifieds), and Allegro (eCommerce).
