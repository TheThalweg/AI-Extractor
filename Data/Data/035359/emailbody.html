Moz down under part 1: Australia deep dive
On his recent trip to Australia, Moz Afzal sat down with Martin Crabb, CIO of Shaw and Partners, for a deep dive into the Australian economy and markets. They discuss how the latest Middle East tensions and higher energy prices are feeding into Australian inflation and interest rates, and the implications for the Aussie dollar. They also explore the outlook for the ASX and why Australiaï¿½s long-term story of population growth, infrastructure demand and a powerful superannuation system still offers compelling opportunities for investors over the next decade.
 
Martin Crabb
Chief Investment Officer, Shaw and Partners
 
Martin Crabb has served as Chief Investment Officer at Shaw and Partners Limited since 2017, having previously held the role of Head of Research from 2011. Martin has a strong focus on small to mid-cap companies. Previous roles include Head of Dealer Services and Head of Product and Research at Macquarie Private Wealth, where responsibilities encompassed adviser support functions and product management. Martin's career at Macquarie Group Limited spanned over two decades, including positions as Executive Director and Head of Macquarie Private Portfolio Management, where Martin contributed to the inception of Individually Managed Accounts for Australian investors. Martin holds a Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics and Economics from the University of Melbourne and a Diploma of Applied Finance and Investment from FINSIA.
