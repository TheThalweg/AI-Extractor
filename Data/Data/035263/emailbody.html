We prefer predictive over descriptive analysis, especially in times of high uncertainty. We thus provide a full-fledged outlook at a time of maximum uncertainty. Our base case remains a near term de-escalation due to high pressure on the US administration. We also provide a forecast for equities and the historical playbook for sectors in case of a "boots on the ground" or a "drag along" scenario.

In our base case, equities recover sharply in Q2. However, the war will leave marks on the economy and on equities in 2026. We reduce our full-year earnings growth forecast from 10-12% to 8-10% and cut our index targets, but we still expect 10% upside for the STOXX 600 in 2026, as of yesterdayï¿½s close. For the scenario of "boots on the ground", we expect no earnings growth and further downside in equity markets, similar to 1990. Back then, it took over 13 months for equity markets to fully recover from the trough. For the "drag along" scenario, we expect only 5% upside through year end. Taking 2022 as an example, it took about three months for equity markets to fully recover from the trough.

Europe vs US: After a stellar outperformance in 2025, we turned neutral on European versus US equities in Q1. However, stronger economic and earnings growth in the US, declining tariffs, better-than-feared economic data, especially in the labour market, combined with low positioning in Tech makes us like US equities tactically better than European equities. Although the initial price performance after a de-escalation of the Iran conflict might be stronger in European cyclicals, we see at least similar upside in US equities in Q2. Also, higher Tech exposure and lower energy prices relative to Europe make the US less sensitive in case of a further escalation of the conflict. We thus see better risk/return in US equities in Q2.

Sectors: In our base case, we expect the sector performance in Q2 to be the mirror image of the performance in Q1. For the remainder of 2026, we expect a much lower divergence in sector performances than in 2025. We reflected this in our sector preferences through our recent upgrades of Software to overweight and by closing our underweight in Staples. For adverse scenarios, see slides 23 and 24.
