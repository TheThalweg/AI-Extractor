Key takeaways
  • We believe that Kering's turnaround remains on track but for now it is largely priced in
  • On our new 2028 EPS estimates (lowered by -4% but still +15% above VA consensus) the stock is trading on 17x PE at the current share price
  • Our DCF implies 15% upside to the shares, which no longer translates into relative outperformance. Hence we move our rating to Equal-weight
  • A continuation of the revamp of the organisation, as well as greater conviction that Gucci's turnaround has legs, could be key catalysts in turning us positive on the shares again

What's new? Kering reports 1Q26 results next week on Tuesday 14th post-market close. The company will be hosting a Capital Market Day the following day in Florence, Italy.

Shares outperformance likely to pause. Year to date, Kering shares have nicely outperformed the Luxury Goods sector, with an outperformance vs. the three largest market capitalizations of the sector (LVMH, Hermï¿½s, Richemont) ranging from +300 bps to +1,700 bps. This is despite a likely softer start to the year top line wise than initially anticipated. On our updated estimates, the stock is currently trading on a 2028e P/E of ~17x and with most of the low hanging fruit already picked (real estate disposal, cosmetics agreement and balance sheet fixing), we see relatively limited upside for the shares for now. We move our rating from Overweight to Equal-weight and lower our price target moderately, from ï¿½330 to ï¿½320 ï¿½ a function of our slightly more conservative top line and margin assumptions over the 2026-28 cycle (see page 2).

We continue to believe that Kering is on the right track, despite a slightly softer than initially anticipated start to the year. We view Luca de Meo joining as Group CEO in September 2025 as a turning point for Kering, which we think will translate to significant operational improvement over the 2026-28 cycle. But given that we already anticipate sales to increase by a cumulated c.+25% (to ï¿½18.2bn) by 2028, consolidated operating margin to grow from 12.5% in 2026 to 18.4% in 2028 and EPS increasing from ï¿½6.81 in 2026 to ï¿½15.97 in 2028 (VA Consensus at ï¿½13.80 in 2028), we feel that it is mostly priced in for now; with our new price target of ï¿½320 implying ~15% upside to the shares, we no longer see Kering as a relative outperformer for now.

What could turn us positive on the shares again?

(1) Continuing with the revamp of the organisation: Following the appointment of Luca de Meo as CEO in September 2025, there has been a reorganisation of reporting lines and creation of new central functions, as well as some executives leaving and joining the Group. Overall, we believe things are moving in the right direction (for example, we share CEO de Meo's view that Kering has been historically essentially a holding company and, as a result, that more can be done to generate synergies within the Group). We see areas where further changes could have a positive impact on the investment case, such as appointing more executives from within the luxury industry to complement talent brought in from outside (so far, most have come from industrial/automotive sector); continuing to promote new talent to the non-Gucci brands' executive committees (which has been done only at the Gucci brand in the last nine months); appointing additional talent in the regions (which has been done selectively in the last nine months).]

(2) Greater conviction that Gucci's turnaround has legs. Overall, we view positively the changes that have taken place at Gucci in recent months from both a creative and management standpoint. Gucci is once again part of the discussion from an aesthetics point of view and Demna is viewed by most of the industry professionals we talk with as one of the leading talents of his generation. That being said, our recent channel checks across European retailers (Luxury - Talk from the trade) suggest the Gucci brand remains a classic case where improving buzz is running ahead of the hard numbers. Feedback around Gucci remains mixed this quarter, with early signs of improving brand buzz but little evidence yet of a meaningful commercial recovery. Overall, the read-through is that Gucciï¿½s brand momentum is improving from very depressed levels, but the business remains in transition, with execution lagging intent. Most experts agree that a turnaround is likely to take time, drawing parallels with Burberry, where recovery required multiple seasons.

Kering CMD: Kering is hosting a CMD in Florence next week starting with a cocktail reception on Wednesday 15th, and followed by a full-day on Thursday 16th which includes a morning presentation followed by 4 "immersion sessions" on eyewear, jewellery, icons, and innovation; after which we will visit a Gucci store, a Ginori store, Saint Laurent l'Atelier maroquinerie and the Gucci Art Lab, ending with a cocktail dinner with Gucci Achives. During the CMD, we don't expect Kering to provide any meaningful financial targets, but instead believe it will focus on strategy and execution in the long run (which we think is the right way for luxury groups to provide guidance).
