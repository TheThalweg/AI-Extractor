Opposing forces for German spreads 		     
 
 
01 April 2026
    Soniya Sadeesh

Although German swap spreads have widened slightly since our last update, the move is still less than implied by the move in credit spreads (or egb spreads) seen over the same period. The rise in foreign official selling of USTs raises the question of whether Euro assets are being disposed as part of the same process. On the flipside, the recent SNB meeting flagged the risk of potential intervention which would imply inflows into euro fixed income.

At this juncture, the main widening risk is likely to stem from fragmentation (ie EGB spread widening), or large scale SNB intervention. Both are plausible outcomes if the oil shock worsens. Given the exogenous nature of the shock, one would expect the ECB to use existing tools to cap any "unwarranted" market fragmentation, which could reduce but not eliminate the widening risk. At the same time, in such a scenario, more FX intervention and a broader fiscal response is also likely which would imply tighter spreads. Taken together, it would suggest being short German ASW should provide a relatively asymmetric risk profile. We maintain existing targets of -22bp on Bund-ï¿½STR and -10bp on Schatz-ï¿½STR.

Over the longer term, recent events reinforce two strategic views which will imply tighter spreads. Firstly the likelihood of diversification away from traditional safe assets, as well the likelihood of higher fiscal expenditure to manage the need for more sovereign strategic independence (be it on national security, energy policy, or even AI).
