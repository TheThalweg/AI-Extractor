We adjust our models for IQVIA trends as well as intra-quarter updates; see Ex. 4-5 for where we expect 1Q beats/misses. Within US large caps, we are OW ABBV/GILD/JNJ/LLY/VRTX and UW BMY.

The macro has dominated recent market dynamics and YTD theDRG/NBI have traded up +2%/+3% vs. S&P500 down -1% as of April 8. Revenue revisions for Large cap Biopharma for 2026-2027 have generally been positive (Exhibit 2) and M&A ($5-$10bn range) has been accelerating, with MRK-TERN (LINK), LLY-CNTA (LINK), GILD (ACLX - LINK, two private companies including Ouro) and BIIB-APLS (LINK).

Earnings focal points:

  • LLY (OW) – See our Pre-preview note HERE. For Zepbound, we model 1Q/2026 sales of $3.95bn/$21.8bn vs. VA cons of $3.95bn/$19.4bn. For Mounjaro US, we model 1Q/2026 sales of $3.8bn/$17.1bn vs. VA cons of $3.7bn/$17.1bn, we see ROW sales as the swing factor (MS/cons $3.4bn/$3.2bn) – see EU GLP-1 IQVIA data HERE. LLY's 2026 revenue guidance is $80bn-$83bn; midpoint of $81.5bn vs. MS/cons of $84.3bn/$81.7bn. EPS guidance is $33.50-$35.00; midpoint of $34.25 vs. MS/cons of $37.35/$34.41. The FDA approved Foundayo (oral GLP-1) on April 1 so early launch commentary on the call (Apr 30) and weekly script data will be the focus – see our tracker HERE. For more on our latest views on the GLP-1 category/TAM and recent patient survey see HERE.
  • ABBV (OW) – We lower our Skyrizi 1Q estimate to $4.5bn vs. guidance of $4.4bn, and we raise Rinvoq to $2.2bn vs. guidance of $2bn given IQVIA trends. We model 1Q revs of $15.1bn vs. guidance of $14.7bn and cons of $14.7bn. Recent launch of JNJ's Icotyde (oral IL23) and potential impact on Skyrizi remain a near-term debate on the stock. We also expect capital-allocation commentary to be a focus for the call given the M&A activity in the space.
  • JNJ (OW) – For 1Q, we model revs of $23.6bn in line with cons of $23.6bn and on the call we look for early commentary on the Icotyde (oral IL23) launch (LINK), and progress with the ongoing Tremfya launch in IBD setting, as well as the myeloma franchise (specifically Tecvayli uptake in earlier lines of MM). We add risk-adjusted sales for Ramantamig (trispecific antibody for MM) and TAR-210 (drug-device combo that releases erdafitinib/FGFRi for bladder cancer) to our model. See our upgrade note HERE.
  • GILD (OW) – We raise our Yeztugo 1Q estimate based on IQVIA trends (see HERE) and model sales of $187mn vs. VA cons: $154mn. We model 2026 Yeztugo sales of $878mn vs. cons of $836mn and GILD guidance of $800mn. We slightly lower Biktarvy for 1Q given IQVIA trends (MS/cons $3.3bn/$3.3bn).
  • VRTX (OW) – For 1Q revs, we model $3bn (7% yoy growth) in line with guidance of ~7%. For the CF franchise, we model 1Q sales of $2.9bn vs. cons. $3.0bn. Recent Pove Ph3 IgAN data represented an important de-risking step for the asset, in our view (see HERE), for EPS season we'll be watching the lateral launch of Otsuka's Voyxact for IgAN (see HERE).
  • BMY(UW) – We update our model for IQVIA data and model 1Q revs of $10.9bn, in line with cons of $10.9bn. We add risk-adjusted sales of Mezigdomide to our model following recent Ph3 MM data (LINK). Late-stage pipeline data in 2H are more important for the stock outlook, and we see Milvexian vs. Eliquis Ph3 afib data as the key (see our prior Moonshot note HERE) and we model a 30% POS.
  • AMGN (EW) – We model 1Q revs of $8.7bn a touch above cons of $8.6bn. The near-term focus is on Repatha and Uplizna, as well as the erosion curve of Prolia. For Repatha 1Q, we model $856mn vs. cons. $830mn. For Uplizna 1Q sales, we model $214mn vs. cons. $196mn. For Prolia, we model $915mn vs. cons. $822mn.On the pipeline front we await MariTide (GLP-GIP) and Olpasiran (Lpa) Ph3 data in 2027.
  • BIIB (EW) – Leqembi Alzheimer's commercial dynamics remain the commercial focus, and we model 1Q sales of $146mn (US $83mn)/vs. VA cons of $148mn (US $82mn). We continue to monitor competitive dynamics vs. LLY's Kisunla. We model 2026 Leqembi sales of $735mn (US $440mn) vs. cons $761mn (US $425mn). On the pipeline side, we are focused on upcoming Ph2 Alzheimer's data for BIIB080 mid-year – see our deep dive HERE.
  • MRK (EW) – We refresh our estimates for IQVIA trends, notably lowering Keytruda for 1Q. We remain focused on pipeline progress (INT cancer program with MRNA – see our Moonshot note HERE, TL1A Ph3 UC data and TROP2 China Ph3 NSCLC data) and capital-allocation commentary.
  • MRNA (EW) – The key focus for the stock is interim Ph3 data for INT (partnered with MRK) in adjuvant melanoma. We think it could potentially happen in 2Q-3Q26; see our preview HERE.
  • PFE (EW) – We refresh our estimates for IQVIA trends and model 1Q revs of $13.8bn vs. cons of $13.9bn. Pipeline catalysts are a focus including SV Ph3 NSCLC data, and PFE plans to present full Ph2 VESPER‑3 results of PF-3944 (monthly injectable GLP-1) along with updated Ph1/2 monotherapy data for MET‑233i (ultra‑long‑acting amylin) at the ADA conference (June 5–8), and potentially an early look at Ph1/2 monthly combo data (GLP+amylin), but this could also come after ADA (LINK).
  • REGN (EW) – For Dupi 1Q, we are ahead of cons., $4.85bn vs. $4.64bn, and generally ahead in the outer years. We model Eylea US 1Q26 sales of $921mn ($483mn for HD vs. $506mn in 4Q25) vs. cons $950mn ($488mn for HD). Next catalyst for the program is FDA action on Eylea HD pre-filled syringe expected in late April. Foundation funding has not returned as of 4/9/26. For HD's IP, we note the inter-parte review (IPR) of a key HD patent – the '036 Patent (2039 expiry) – was recently granted by the PTAB, and we expect a ruling by 3/3/2027. See inside for additional details on HD's IP. Fianlimab (LAG3) Ph3 melanoma data represent the next pipeline catalyst – see our thoughts HERE.
  • UTHR (EW) – Following recent Ph3 trial success of Tyvaso in IPF and ralinepag in PAH, we raise our POS and our PT goes to $519.

We also update our models for 10-K's and adjust the discount period in our large-cap biotech models to align with our smid-cap models. See within for detailed changes.
