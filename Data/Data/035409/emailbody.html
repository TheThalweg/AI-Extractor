1) Amadeus - Flexing scenarios for Middle East disruption and potential spillovers(*MS Research*, #Amadeus): We published a three-scenario analysis for potential Middle East disruption and how it could impact Amadeus' near-term numbers. Amadeus is the global leader in travel technology. Focusing on the base case ï¿½ 'ongoing constraints'. We estimate Amadeus has roughly 9% revenue exposure to the Middle East and expect disruption to have kicked in from March. Rolling through potential outcomes for the remainder of 2026, we now forecast 5.3% constant currency revenue growth for FY26, down from ~8%, with mid-single digit EPS cuts. In our view, that means FY26 guidance for high-single digit % cc revenue growth is at risk. Although we see a tricky tactical setup into 1Q results on May 8th, we like the medium-term story with the stock on 13x 2027e P/E. In our recent Software Moat note, Amadeus screened second highest out of our coverage, benefiting from its running of mission-critical systems, network effects in the distribution business, and has clear market leadership across segments. We have an Overweight rating on Amadeus ï¿½ let us know if you would like to discuss. See our full note here.

2) US colleague Chris Quintero talks to AI-native ERP startup Rillet's CEO & Co-Founder (*MS Research*, #Sage, #SAP): Rillet was founded in 2021 to "replace 20th century accounting software with AI-native ERP", mainly targeting the mid-market segment. Rillet raised $70m in a Series B round co-led by Andreessen Horowitz and ICONIQ in August 2025, in which it cited that the company had over 200 customers. Our US colleague Chris Quintero sat down with Rillet CEO Nicolas Kopp, and discussed topics including "Why is now the time to disrupt?", "Who is the competition?", "Where can AI be used in accounting?", "How are you leveraging AI agents?" Watch the interview here. Mr Kopp called out NetSuite as their primary incumbent competitor, but Sage is also a relevant player in the US mid-market (mainly through modern cloud solution Sage Intacct, and to a lesser extent, Sage 100 and 300). The discussion highlights the growing role of AI in modernising accounting workflows and underscores the depth of competition across the mid-market financial software landscape. For Sage, Intacct's ability to sustain strong growth in its core US market, and internationalise are key debates. While Rillet is US-based, we also highlight our overview of European private startups: Scaling in the Shadows.

3) 1Q26 CIO Survey - IT budgets tick up, App vendors' AI relevance rises (*MS Research*, #Software, #IT Services): Our survey indicated 2026 IT budget growth is expected to accelerate to 3.7% (+10bps y/y), versus our prior survey of 3.4% (-10bps y/y). However, near-term budget revision expectations still skew to the downside. AI remains the top CIO priority and among the most macro-defensive IT project type. With regards to assisting the deployment of AI at the enterprise level, the results show that application vendors are now broadly used as hyperscalers. Individual vendors gaining the largest incremental share of GenAI spend in 2026 are charted below, with the foundational AI labs ticking up, as did (on the app software side) Salesforce and ServiceNow. Lastly, only 20% of CIO responses were collected post the outbreak of the Middle East conflict (28 Feb), but these did not show a deterioration in growth expectations. Still, we see some risks into 1Q for Software companies with back-end loaded quarters (e.g. SAP, Dassault, Temenos), and more broadly with regards to tone around FY outlooks (particularly in IT services side, given consulting work tends to be relatively cyclical). 4) OVH Groupe raises capex guidance on memory component and disk price inflation (#IONOS): French cloud service provider OVH raised its capex guidance on Thursday, with the direct FY26 impact (net of mitigations) prompting a c. 3pps increase in FY26 guided capex (to 33-35% of sales). The news contributed to a 6% stock decline on the day. Specifically, OVH called out "exceptional cost inflation in memory components and disks." This is a relevant dynamic for IONOS, albeit Cloud Solutions is only c. 14% of IONOS' revenue. We also think IONOS will have done its own mitigations, that its capex guidance for FY26 is relatively well-set and we are not expecting revision risks here heading into results. Separately, OVH's 2Q26 revenue growth remained muted at 5.1% L4L, slightly slower than 1Q26 and suggesting a still tough demand environment in Europe.

5) TCS 4Q26 - In-line quarter, mixed 4Q regional trends, balanced outlook commentary (#IT Services): TCS (covered by Gaurav Rateria) closed out its FY26 (March-end) with an in-line 4Q print. In the mix, North America cc y/y growth improved to 2.5% (from 1.3% in 3Q), the UK decline moderated to -1.2% cc (vs. -3.2% in 3Q and in the context of also negative growth in 1H), and Continental Europe y/y cc growth moderated slightly to 1.0% (from 1.4%). Gaurav wrote: "...management commentary appeared relatively balanced, citing hope for a better F27 vs. F26 but also pointing to macro uncertainty continuing to affect decision-making across key verticals such as [Banking and Financial Services]". Management also flagged that enterprise AI adoption shifted from experimentation to scaled deployment in FY26, and that customers are increasingly confident about putting AI into production, while emphasizing the importance of security, governance, and guardrails.
