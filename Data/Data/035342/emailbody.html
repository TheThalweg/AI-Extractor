Global Markets Daily: Asian FX Outlook Post Ceasefire and Implications of India's FX Regulations
10 April 2026 | 5:57PM SGT | Research | Economics

●

Asian curves bear-flattened significantly last month as the market repriced inflation expectations. After the announcement of a ceasefire in the Middle East on 8th April, front-end Asian rates fell sharply (by around 5-30bp across the region). Even after the correction, we think that most Asian curves are still pricing in too many hikes (~2-3 hikes in Korea, ~3 hikes in India, ~1.5 hikes in Thailand) versus our forecasts.

●

We assessed that the Asian economies most exposed to higher oil prices are likely to be the Philippines, Thailand and India. Their respective currencies were the regional underperformers in March. KRW also underperformed, which we attribute to the broader 'risk off' sentiment and drawdown in Korean equities. The reaction in Asian FX markets on the day of the ceasefire announcement was broadly just a reversal of the prior underperformers.

●

We remain most constructive on the CNY and the MYR. China's diversified energy sources, export outperformance and still-significant undervaluation underpin our bullish CNY view. The MYR is also still undervalued (vs GS DEER), is a net energy exporter, and positioning is likely cleaner than pre-war. We remain bearish on the PHP, THB and INR, given their exposure to energy imports and relatively weak BoP/growth outlooks.

●

India has tightened FX regulations meaningfully over the past fortnight, by capping authorized dealers' end-of-day net open position at US$100mn, a sharp reduction from the prior limit of around 20%-25% of Tier I and II capital (in some cases US$1bn+ for foreign banks in India), and restricted authorized dealers from offering INR non-deliverable forwards (NDFs) to residents or non-residents.

●

In our view, these steps are aimed at curbing speculative INR short positioning, particularly “FX carry trades” that buy USD/INR deliverable forwards (DF) onshore, and simultaneously selling USD/INR NDFs at the same maturity. As a consequence, spot USD/INR and both DF and NDF outright curves shifted lower as dealers unwound the carry trades.

●

Given the 10th April deadline for dealers to adhere to the rules, we think the position unwind is mostly complete. However, these measures do not resolve the underlying external backdrop. As such, we continue to expect INR underperformance versus NJA peers from current levels.
