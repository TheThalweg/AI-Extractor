Guidance Update: high teens revenue growth; +LSD% SSSG for both China & US markets; OPM may contract but narrower than 1 pp in 2026

The key takeaway from Miniso's analyst briefing is the positive SSSG guidance at +LSD% for both the China and US markets. Conversely, a slight headwind is the continuous margin pressure, primarily due to the expansion of directly-operated stores, but OPM contraction this year should be controlled within 1 pp, narrower compared to 2.9 pp in 2025.

We maintain our Buy rating on Miniso. We view the current valuation as an attractive entry point for a double-digit growth company in the China Consumer space, which now also offers a decent dividend yield.After a sharp correction of the stock (-13.6% YTD; HSI: - 3.29%), Miniso's current share price implies a 10.8x 2026E P/E and a 4.7% dividend yield. Over a three-year horizon (2025-2028E), we project an 11% EPS CAGR on the back of a 13% sales CAGR. Furthermore, a potential turnaround at Yonghui could serve as a 'call option' on the stock.

For the 1Q26, management anticipates that the sales trend will remain robust. SSSG is estimated to reach HSD% in China and double-digit % in the US. On profit growth outlook, management thinks the trend will accelerate in 2H26 considering the base effect.

4Q25/FY25 financial result highlights:
  • Revenue: 4Q25 revenue growth accelerated to 32.7% YoY, beating Bloomberg consensus by 3%. SSSG reached +MSD% during the period. By brand, Miniso revenue rose 27.7% YoY, while Top Toy surged 111.8% YoY. For FY25, revenue increased 26.2% YoY, with the company level achieving +LSD SSSG ( China outperforming overseas market).
  • Adjusted operating Profit: 4Q25 adjusted operating profit grew 11.7% YoY. This is slower than revenue growth due to a higher contribution from lower-margin, directly operated regions during the Western holiday season. Adjusted OPM contracted by 3.2 pp YoY to 17% (vs. 17.6% in 3Q25). FY25 adjusted operating profit rose 7.9% YoY, with an operating margin of 17.1% ( -2.9 pp from 20% in FY24).
  • Adjusted Net Profit: 4Q25 adjusted net profit increased 7.6% YoY to RMB 853mn, representing a 13.6% adjusted net margin (-3.2 ppts YoY; 13.5% in 3Q25). While this was 1% below the BBG consensus of RMB 860mn, we believe it is better than market concerns of a much lower forecast. FY25 adjusted net profit rose 6.5% to RMB 2,898mn, with a margin of 13.5% (down from 16% in FY24).
  • Reported Net Profit: 4Q25 saw a record loss of RMB 142mn due to non-recurring items related to the Yonghui acquisition, compared to a net profit of RMB 806mn in 4Q24.
  • SSSG trend: In 2025, Miniso achieved +MSD% for China business ,and -LSD% for overseas business. Among overseas markets, Asia (ex-China) and Latin America are the worst performing regions, with the SSSG declined by MSD%. US grew by +MSD%, while Europe also also derived a +LSD% SSSG in 2026.
2026 operational updates:
  • Chinese market: In 2M26, GMV grew over 25% YoY, with same-store GMV recording +HSD YoY. In 1Q26, China business is expected to deliver +HSD% SSSG.
  • North America market: In 2M26,GMV surged by more than 50% YoY, and same-store GMV increased by at least 20% YoY. In 1Q26, US business is expected to have +double digit SSSG.
  • Operating margin trend by business mix: OPM contraction is primarily driven by Indonesia and the Philippines, where a weak macro environment and earlier expansion have created operating deleverage. By business mix, the lower-margin, directly-operated stores are growing faster than the higher-margin franchise business. Directly-operated stores are expected to contribute 30% of revenue in 2026 (up from 20%).
  • Profitability update by regions: China's OPM should remain stable, while the overseas franchise business will see a slight OPM decline. Directly-operated businesses in the US & Europe are expected to improve on better cost control. Management thinks that the overall OPM contraction should be less than 1 pp, improving from 2.9 pp contraction in 2025.
