DB View

Inditex reported a strong FY26 with 6% EPS growth based on 7% cFX despite a -4% FX headwind. The current trading for the first period of 1Q at 9% supports a HSD% (or even DD%) growth rate in 1Q in our view. The global macro headwinds will likely see an impact on consumer demand given inflation and costs will come under pressure from air freight hikes. However, with an agile business model, flexible sourcing, dynamic pricing and a high EBIT margin, we see Inditex as well placed to navigate these challenges.

We update our numbers post the FY results with no material changes to changes to our EPS (-1% EPS) but a reduction to FCF given higher capex spend. The shares are down -13% YTD with the stock now trading on c.22x Cal 26e PE and we re-iterate our Buy recommendation although with a ï¿½60 PT (from ï¿½63) given higher capex guidance than we expected.

Attractive long term dynamics despite short term headwinds

As a global retailer Inditex will likely always see some risk of geo-political uncertainty but the sales mix is well diversified which limits the downside risk. With a single global inventory pool and a short stock turn there is opportunity to sell product into different regions in the same season which limits markdown risk. However, compared to other retailers there is a more significant use of air freight (inbound into Spain and more importantly outbound from Spain) and this will have a potential margin impact. c.20% of sales are in Asia which we assume has seen a c.50% increase in air freight and c.20% in the Americas which we assume has seen a c.20% increase.
