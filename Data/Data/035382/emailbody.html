Marchï¿½s headline inflation figures came in above expectations, bringing the annual rate to 4.14% y/y from 3.81% in February. Core inflation surprised slightly on the upside at 4.78% y/y (from 4.92% in the previous month).

Largest contribution came from food & beverages and transport items. Gasoline prices increased by 4.6% m/m, and diesel prices by 13.9% m/m.

While the headline inflation print was higher than anticipated, the underlying composition was favorable, with both core and services inflation decelerating.

Inflation in April and May will reflect ongoing direct upward pressure from fuel prices and its pass-through to other items. Elevated diesel costs will continue to feed into agricultural prices.

We project annual headline and core inflation to close 2026 at 4.86% and 4.19%, respectively. We expect headline inflation to hover above 4% for the remainder of 2026.

Given the relatively benign inflation composition, we do not see the stronger-than-projected print derailing our expected 25bp cut in the April Copom.
