The January GDP report disappointed ï¿½ plain and simple. With the economy stagnating to start the year, we expect a rebound in February. We don't discount an upward revision to January GDP either. Our nowcast models point to both a potential upward revision to January and some further upward momentum in February.

What do we see for February GDP? We see GDP expanding by 0.2% m-o-m, lifted by broad-based momentum across the services, production and construction sectors. We think risks around our nowcasts are balanced.

Looking ahead, we expect growth to temper. Indeed, higher uncertainty would dampen spending and investment. Tighter financial conditions won't help either. With sentiment weakening, we expect output to also take a hit. We recently updated our economic projections. We see Q2-26 GDP growth slowing to 0.2% q-o-q (from 0.3% q-o-q in Q1-26). We see 2026 GDP at 0.7%.

The recent Middle-East truce remains fragile. While energy prices have retreated from their highs, oil and gas prices remain well above their pre-conflict levels. Importantly, we don't expect the economy to return to its pre-conflict level. And despite some diminishing downside risks to the economic outlook, we continue to think that risks to activity are still skewed negatively, relative to our central projections.
