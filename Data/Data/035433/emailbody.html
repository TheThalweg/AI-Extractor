a basic directional framework:

 

i. one takeaway from this past week: the deep left tail scenario should be bounded.

 

ii. with that said, judging from developments this weekend, the noise (and endless game theory) continues.

 

iii. technical factors are supportive -- flows, seasonals, liquidity -- but they constitute a guarantee of nothing when the trading environment is this fickle.

 

iv. I suspect that Q1 earnings will be a net positive, with a specific window of opportunity for mega cap tech companies to step into the breach.

 

v. net of everything above and below, I expect that S&P will remain in a volatile trading range, where you�re supposed to fade at the edges (as Dom Wilson put it, build longs on pullbacks / add protection on rips).

On the other side of these purchases of macro products we saw rather aggressive reshorting of single stocks � yes most notably software (again). More detail on this below. 

 

All 3 momentum thresholds for CTA community are now firmly in positive territory. We think CTAs bought ~$10b of S&P 500 last week and are set to buy an additional ~$30b this week. This cohort is currently in the process of flipping from short to long which will continue to act as a market tailwind at the index level.

 

The Long Only community dipped their toes back into the water after staying on the sidelines since the war started. Passive yet consistent purchases in what was working before the war � mainly semi caps & memory. We saw fresh demand in the Mag 7 complex with continued supply in software on the other side of the equation. We think a majority of investors prefer to 'wait' for any re-acceleration in Software businesses before re-engaging on the long-side.

 

In-line with a return to popular longs and shorts, our High Beta Momentum Pair had its best two-day swing since Covid last week. Now momentum increasingly looks like the AI trade, with correlation of our High Beta Momo (GSPRHIMO) and AI (GSPUARTI) pairs are at the highs. Despite these latest moves, momentum is NOT yet overbought and if earnings fundamentals remain solid, the current breakout could continue. (H/T Lou Miller)
