Hungary will go to polling stations on April 12th to vote in the crucial general elections, where the incumbent Fidesz government is set to face one of the strongest electoral challenges in years. While opinion poll results can be quite divergent, most opinion polls currently suggest that the opposition Tisza party has a strong lead going into the elections, and is more likely to win majority seats than the Fidesz-KDNP coalition. That said, the complicated parliamentary structure, increased mobilisation among Fidesz voters on election day, and possibility of actual results diverging from opinion polls mean that the election could be much closer.

Ahead of the elections, we will be holding a webinar featuring our CEEMEA Research and Senior Trading teams to discuss the likely outcomes, risks, and implications for markets.
