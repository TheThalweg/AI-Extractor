Flight Deck

Retirements up in the air
christophe.menard@db.com
06-04-2026

The ongoing conflict in Iran is expected to keep oil prices high for an extended period, leading to jet fuel availability bottlenecks globally. While $70/barrel allowed operation of both old and new engines, prices over $100 are problematic, especially as they are now likely to persist. This sustained high oil price environment could trigger a reversal in the trend of aircraft retirements, which are currently at all-time lows (around 0.3% of the fleet in Q4 2025). Such a shift would boost the supply of Used Serviceable Material (USM), currently experiencing a shortage. Aircraft most at risk of early retirement are those 25 years or older, representing approximately 4,200 engines. Of these, 50% are CFM56 and CF6 models.

Universal Music

Takeover offer for UMG
silvia.cuneo@db.com
07-04-2026

Pershing Square Capital Management has put forward a non-binding proposal to acquire all outstanding shares of UMG through a business combination. The transaction would involve UMG merging with Pershing Square SPARC Holdings, Ltd., resulting in a new entity, "New UMG," which would be a Nevada corporation listed on the New York Stock Exchange. UMG shareholders are offered a total package comprising EUR 9.4bn in cash (EUR 5.05 per share) and 0.77 shares in New UMG stock for each UMG share held. According to the press release, this total consideration is valued at EUR 30.40 per share, representing a 78% premium to UMG's closing price on the 2nd of April. Shareholders will have options to elect all cash, all stock, or a mix, subject to proration. As a reminder, Pershing is the top six shareholder in UMG with a stake of c. 4.5% of shares outstanding and has been invested since four and a half years ago.

Safran

A resilient profile, with a solid Q1 ahead
christophe.menard@db.com
08-04-2026

Safran is expected to release its Q1 2026 results on April 23, 2026, with a conference call scheduled for 8:30 AM CET on the same day. A solid Q1 in sight - FX constitutes a headwind in the quarter, with a 10% drag. Regarding seasonality and quarterly performance in propulsion, Safran has already indicated it expects a strong start to Q1 in spare parts. We believe the company is likely seeking to unlock supply chain bottlenecks for CFM56 spare parts, which would further support performance in 2026. Hence, our view of spare part growth at 18% (above the mid-teen guidance for the year ) and service revenues up 20%. Both assumptions could prove conservative. As for LEAP deliveries, we expect a surge in Q1-26 to approximately 50%, as Q1-25 had been weak on that front (319 deliveries) and Q4-25 deliveries at 562 units had some catch-up embedded.

Chemicals

Chart of the day: Beware of the cost squeeze!
virginie.boucher-ferte@db.com
09-04-2026

The sector is broadly flat since the start of the conflict, despite growing demand and cost headwinds. Whilst we believe upstream chemicals, distributors and gases may see some net benefits in Q2, we struggle to see the positives for most downstream businesses. We believe the market is too complacent, drawing too much of a parallel with 2022, when industrial specialty chemical businesses over-earned due to strong pricing, well above the rate of cost inflation. We see the situation as radically different today, due to the global chemical oversupply (which reduces customers' fear of shortages), weaker industrial demand (pricing power is a function of demand) and already squeezed consumers further down the chain.

Critical infrastructure

02-04-2026

Conflict escalating in the Middle East and continuing in Ukraine is elevating risks to inflation, rates and yields in the UK. In this context, we expect the infrastructure construction sub-sector to continue to outperform the wider construction sector and UK equity market, for the reasons highlighted below. We reiterate our Buy recommendations for Balfour Beatty, Kier and Morgan Sindall. Aggregate order books across the sub-sector grew 14% in FY25 to a record level, covering 2.4x year's revenue (5-year average: 2.2x). Orders are only included in order books where they are secured, with funding in place, and there is a much larger additional pipeline of preferred bidder work and framework positions providing long-term visibility and repeatable routes-to-market.

UBS

Questions of time
benjamin.goy@db.com
Published on 8th April 2026

This month is anticipated to bring more regulatory clarity regarding Swiss "Too Big To Fail" (TBTF) reforms. While the impending ordinance may lean towards the more stringent end of expectations, a potential for compromise on foreign subsidiary regulation still exists. Overall, final regulatory clarity will take more time but is important for the bank as well as shareholders, and will allow management to adapt, at least to the foreign subsidiary proposal. Despite the prospect of substantial trapped capital, we believe UBS is well-positioned to manage capital build requirements alongside increasing capital returns, driven by enhanced profitability and business adjustments. In the interim, we forecast another solid quarterly performance as the bank continues its strong execution. The Iran conflict has not yet negatively impacted our forecasts, though this could change over time. Our EPS forecasts remain largely stable, reflecting a balance between downgrades in Global Wealth Management and upgrades in the Investment Bank.

Diageo

All about the rebase
mitch.collett@db.com
Published on 31st March 2026

We upgrade Diageo to BUY with a 12m price target of 1,650p, suggesting 20% upside. Our estimates reflect a reset to profitability in FY27 and below guidance delivery in FY26 which leaves our EPS estimates 11% below consensus in FY27 and 10% below in FY28. However, we believe this quantum of downgrade is already reflected in the share price. On our 12m PT the shares would trade on 14.7x P/E, a 13% / 30% discount to European Staples / Diageo's 10yr average P/E (20.7x). Industry headwinds and market-share loss mean we expect Diageo to reduce profitability to drive competitiveness. We assume a 600bps/200bps reduction to EBIT margins in North America / Europe, driving a 250bps cut to group EBIT margins in FY27. We believe the company can invest in price, marketing, route to market and innovation leaving Diageo better able to deliver the sort of predictable profitable growth that investors value highly.
