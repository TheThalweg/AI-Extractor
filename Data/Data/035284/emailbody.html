Q1 26: A strong finish to the quarter

Registrations in March came in at 1.4mn units, +10% y/y (source: GlobalData) in W. Europe and wrapped up the first quarter with volumes just under 3.2mn units, +4%. March benefitted from an extra working day this year (~+5% impact) and also is a high volume month in the region, especially in the UK with the plate change that occurs. Q1 volumes are about 17% lower than the volumes seen back in Q1 19 (March -13% vs. March 2019). SAAR dropped to 12.2mn/yr from 12.8mn/yr in February with the Q1 run rate averaging just under 12.1mn/yr. We do not find a significant correlation between oil price moves and European demand (see note) but cannot rule out that longer-term higher inflation will either weigh on volumes or mix in the region. We anticipate that the W. European car market will reach just over 11.9mn units this year, resulting in another marginal gain in registrations, while production could be flat to slightly down (see outlook). The European Auto Manufacturers' Association (ACEA) is due to report March registrations on 23rd April 2026.
