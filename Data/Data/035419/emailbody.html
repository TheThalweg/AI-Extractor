Weekly Warm-up: The Market Waits for No One

North America
Michael J Wilson, Andrew B Pauker, Michelle M. Weaver, CFA, Diane Ding, Ph.D., Nicholas Lentini, CFA

April 13, 2026
Equity markets trade in the future where information is imperfect and uncertain. Just like they discounted much of the uncertainty we are now seeing in the headlines, they are now looking ahead to resolution of that uncertainty and better visibility on the rolling recovery that began a year ago.
  • The Market Waits for No One...We enter the week with markets that still feel fragile to many market participants but are likely further along in the discounting process than appreciated. In fact, as discussed in our Weekly Warm-Up last Monday, the S&P 500 was in the process of carving out a low after hitting and holding the low end of our targeted range of 6300-6500 for this correction. So far, so good with the S&P 500 up another 3.5% last week and 7% from the lows just 2 weeks ago. We continue to favor leaning into a barbell of (1) cyclicals where earnings remain strong and valuations have compressed (Financials, Industrials, Consumer Discretionary Goods), coupled with quality growth where sentiment and valuations have also reset to healthy levels (the hyperscalers). With peace talks stalling over the weekend and central banks remaining focused on inflation risk, equity markets may need to back and fill and even retest our targeted range again. That said, our advice is to be ready to add risk into this, because the market waits for no one.
  • A Strengthening Earnings Backdrop Continues to Anchor Our Views...S&P 500 trailing earnings growth is now 15%, and next twelve month earnings is up over 20% Y/Y. Earnings revisions breadth (currently 8%) has remained resilient since the Iran war started, and the 1Q and 2Q 2026 EPS estimates for the S&P are up since the end of February (+1% and +4%, respectively). Leading indicators point to a continuation of positive operating leverage, and AI adoption is a margin tailwind. The earnings upside isn't just limited to the S&P 500. The S&P 600 Small Cap Index earnings growth is also strong at 14%. We are encouraged that earnings revisions breadth for many cyclical areas of the market (including Cap Goods, Materials, Consumer Durables & Apparel, and Retailing) has improved since the end of February even amid the geopolitical risks. See our 1Q earnings preview section for more detail.
  • Have Energy Prices Already Peaked? We have contended that the solution for high commodity prices is high commodity prices as, eventually, demand is curbed and/or production picks up. We also believe resiliency is elevated in times like this, and that countries will find ways to solve a problem that is intolerable for the global economy, much like they did in 2022 when oil prices spiked and natural gas prices reached excessively high levels in Europe. The extreme backwardation is the result of severe shortages that are viewed as temporary for now, and in the absence of a permanent supply shock. From an equity standpoint, energy stocks' relative performance also appears to have peaked and turned lower. Thus, the market is signaling lower oil and gas prices between today and year end, and perhaps materially so.
  • The Rebalancing Continues as Private Economy Takes the Baton from Government...In our year-ahead outlook we laid out our view that the administration's policies are designed to rebalance the economy on 3 planes simultaneously. We are pleased to report that there is now evidence that some of this rebalancing is happening. Today, we offer a fourth leg to the stool where the objective is to shift growth from the public to the private sector. Recent jobs data supports that view with private employment showing relative strength, something that will further enhance the other legs, in our view.
