High Level Thoughts Coming into 1Q26 Earnings

While we acknowledge that the risk in the environment is elevated, we still have strong conviction about each of the key tailwinds for the group, many of which are already coming through and should be evident at earnings.

Key tailwinds include: 1) Increased clarity on capital rules supporting higher excess capital deployment into loan growth, capital markets, and buybacks; 2) Resilient capital markets activity, especially in trading; 3) Net interest margin expansion even before the Fed cuts again (especially for the regional and super-regional banks); 4) Accelerating loan growth with more runway from here; and 5) Stable credit quality.

We believe that as the macro uncertainty fades, the group will outperform as they generate strong earnings performance by deploying excess capital into higher loan growth, capital markets activity, and buybacks. We expect consensus estimates for 2027/28 need to move higher as the street bakes in excess capital deployment.

The median bank stock in our coverage is trading at 11.4x Consensus NTM EPS (11.7x for Large Cap Banks and 10.8x for Midcap Banks) versus 12.5x in February. We think full year guidance is still achievable for most banks, so lower valuations set up an attractive risk-reward into earnings as banks reaffirm their outlooks.

Most Preferred Names into the Quarter

Zions Bancorporation (ZION, OW): Upside to Street estimates for operating leverage in 2026, and growing flexibility for capital return.

Huntington (HBAN, OW): More clarity on the path to $1.90-1.93 EPS target for 2027, including flexibility on revenue-related expenses and buybacks.

Goldman Sachs (GS, EW): Biggest benefit in the group from strong trading volumes. We model Equities up +25% y/y (Financing +35%, Intermediation +20%) and Fixed Income up +19% y/y (Financing +15%, Intermediation +20%).

UMB Financial (UMBF, OW): Expect 2Q26 NIM guidance to surprise positively on the earnings call, with commentary pointing to core NIM expansion over the next 12 months which is not fully baked into Consensus.

Other Thoughts to Highlight

Given the selloff across the group, we are assuming that several banks leaned in more on buybacks in 1Q with their excess capital. This could continue into 2Q if volatility persists.

Expect private credit to be a big talking point on earnings calls. We view bank lending to private credit as more of a headline risk than a fundamental risk, given the low exposure (median 2% of loans) and the significant safeguards that banks have baked into their loan structures (e.g., conservative advance rates, senior positioning, and influence over collateral).
