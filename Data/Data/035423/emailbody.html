The Q1 earnings season is set to start amidst a challenging macro backdrop and geopolitical uncertainties. Eurozone PMIs were mixed in Q1, and the weaker USD likely presented a key headwind to European earnings. Despite that, Q1 earnings estimates were revised upwards over the past weeks, and consensus now expects Q1 earnings to have grown by 2% yoy. We see limited scope for positive surprises and project 3% aggregate earnings growth, implying a beat rate well below the long-term average. Looking at the full year, we have revised down our earnings growth forecast to reflect higher energy prices and geopolitical uncertainty. We now project 8-10% earnings growth this year, roughly in line with consensus.

We expect below-average earnings beats in the STOXX 600
