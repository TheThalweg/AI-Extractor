Americas Restaurants: CY1Q26 Preview: Bracing for stepped-up volatility and wider divergence amidst a softer macro backdrop
10 April 2026 | 4:39PM EDT | Research | Equity

Earlier this week, we lowered our 2026 discretionary cash inflow expectations for US consumer to +4.2% in 2026 (vs. our previous expectation of +5.1% as of Jan '26) to reflect incremental headwinds from rising oil prices.

While restaurant industry SSSG have not exhibited a meaningful correlation to fuel prices, we find strong (statistically significant) relationships between SSS trends with broader macro growth (e.g., real GDP) and hence expect a more challenging top-line backdrop in the balance of 2026, partially offsetting the stimulus and tax refund benefits. Our catch-ups with the companies in the last few weeks also broadly points to increased macro related uncertainties although it is difficult to gauge the magnitude of the impact so far given inclement weather (i.e., Storm Fern and Hernando in Jan/Feb) and calendar shifts in 1Q26.

To reflect this, we are broadly lowering our FY26-28 EBITDA/EPS estimates and 12-m TPs across our coverage, with a few exceptions like CAVA and QSR where are folding in company-specific drivers into our assumptions. That said, we would continue to lean into stocks with idiosyncratic growth drivers, including companies driving product/marketing innovation and taking share from peers in their category (BROS, DPZ, EAT, MCD, YUM) and/or making progress on operational turnarounds (SHAK, QSR).
