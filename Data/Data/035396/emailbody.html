This publication is a compilation of key research reports published over the past week.

Financial conditions eased over the week with the tentative Middle East cease fire. The market is pricing unchanged Fed policy through October and a trough in SOFR around 3.4% late next year. Measures of the neutral real rate are centered on 1.6%, implying that Fed policy is currently accommodative given trend inflation of around 3% (see here and here). Relatedly, absent concerns about the impact of AI on the labor market and the Iran war, economic data do not justify further Fed rate cuts. We continue to see term premia rising amid the ongoing supply shock and structurally easier fiscal policy. Forward breakevens continue to point to well-anchored long-run inflation expectations, though we are wary of the signal from 5y5y. Our views on swap spreads and funding markets are unchanged.
