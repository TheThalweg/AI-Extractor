US Weekly Kickstart: The de-rating of secular growth
10 April 2026 | 5:27PM EDT | Research | Portfolio Strategy

●

While the S&P 500 has rebounded to within 2% of its record high, secular growth stocks remain more than 20% below their highs of October 2025. Secular growth stocks have faced a series of challenges in recent months, including expectations of economic acceleration, surging bond yields, and AI disruption risk. The median stock in our secular growth screen has experienced a 30% P/E compression during the past few months and now trades at nearly the lowest valuation multiple in a decade. Across the market, stocks with elevated revenue growth have broadly suffered contracting valuations in recent months.

●

The macro outlook going forward appears supportive for secular growth stocks. Historically, secular growth stocks have outperformed when economic growth was modest and bond yields were falling. Our economists expect US real GDP growth of roughly 1.7% in the second half of this year, and our rates strategists expect Treasury yields will gradually decline through year-end.

●

However, AI uncertainty is unlikely to abate in the near future, arguing for a persistently higher discount rate and a selective approach to secular growth. We refresh our "Rule of 10" screen of secular growth stocks, which identifies S&P 500 companies with realized and expected annual sales growth of at least 10% in each year from 2024 through 2028. A record 35 stocks currently screen into the list, with 11 from the Software industry and a number of others with exposure to AI investment and uncertainty (Exhibit 15).

●

Even excluding Software, many secular growth stocks have recently underperformed and trade at discounted valuation multiples relative to the past decade. The median non-Software stock in our "Rule of 10" secular growth screen trades at a P/E of 29x, a 53% premium to the median S&P 500 stock that is close to the bottom of the range during the past 10 years. Consensus 2027 sales growth for the median company in the screen is 3x the growth rate for the median S&P 500 company. PEG ratios are also similar to levels reached during recent troughs. Among secular growth themes, we continue to think companies tied to investment in power infrastructure are a particularly attractive area (Exhibit 16).
