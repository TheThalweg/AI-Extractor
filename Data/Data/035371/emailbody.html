USA: UMich Sentiment Declines Sharply; Inflation Expectations Rise; Factory Orders Slightly Above Expectations

Year-ahead inflation expectations increased sharply, and 5-10 year inflation expectations also increased. The University of Michigan noted that ï¿½98% of interviews were completed prior to the April 7th announcement of a temporary ceasefireï¿½ and that ï¿½Economic expectations will likely improve after consumers gain confidence that the supply disruptions stemming from the Iran conflict have ended and gas price have moderated.ï¿½ Factory orders were flat in February, against expectations for a decline. MAIN POINTS:

1. The University of Michiganï¿½s index of consumer sentiment declined by 5.7pt to 47.6 in the April preliminary report, below expectations and the lowest level in the surveyï¿½s history if it holds in the final report. The survey's current economic conditions component declined by 5.7pt to 50.1, which would also be the lowest level in the surveyï¿½s history if it holds in the final report, and the consumer expectations component declined by 5.6pt to 46.1, the lowest level since May 1980. Interviews were conducted between March 24 and April 7, and the University of Michigan noted that ï¿½98% of interviews were completed prior to the April 7th announcement of a temporary ceasefireï¿½ and that ï¿½Economic expectations will likely improve after consumers gain confidence that the supply disruptions stemming from the Iran conflict have ended and gas price have moderated.ï¿½

2. Median inflation expectations over the next year rose 1.0pp to 4.8% in April, well above consensus expectations and the highest since August 2025. Inflation expectations over the next 5-10 years increased by 0.2pp to 3.4% in April, in line with consensus expectations.

3. Factory orders were flat in February, against expectations for a decline. Growth in factory orders was revised down by 0.1pp to flat in January. Growth in both core capital goods orders and shipments was revised up 0.1pp in February, to +0.7% and +1.0%, respectively. Growth in both durable goods orders and durable goods orders ex-transportation was revised up by 0.1pp in February, to -1.3% and +0.9%, respectively.

4. We left our Q1 GDP tracking estimate unchanged at +2.8% (quarter-over-quarter annualized).
