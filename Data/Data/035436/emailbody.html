Emerging Markets Morning Pulse

 

 

 

Global Emerging Markets credit had a fairly strong session on Friday with CEEMEA outperforming the Latam space. Turkey saw a bounce across the corporate space (0.5pts) with offer side liquidity being shallow. MENA saw a bounce in high beta (+0.5-1pt) while the broader IG space was up by +0.25pts. LATAM $ sovereign credit finished wrapped around unchanged with MEX/PEMEX lagging while COLOM/ECOPET felt firm. Main story of the day was AEGEBZ which opened -8pts lower following another delay to their result publication. The dip was bought aggressively after the company announced there are purely operational reasons and we closed +4pts off the lows. This morning after failed peace talks over the weekend we are opening up to -0.5pts lower across the Asia HY space. IG bonds are opening +3-4bps wider with core rates also trading +2-3bps wider this morning. We expect CEEMEA $ credit to open moderately lower. Early morning flows are two-way and we are far from any kind of selloff.

 

*** Rates ***

💰 US Treasury yields closed higher on Friday on back of US President Trump‘s threats to escalate the Iran conflict should weekend talks fail while the highest US CPI print in four years, even though in line with consensus, shifted rate cut bets lower. The yield curve closed +3-4bps wider in a parallel shift. UST yields jumped this morning following failed Iran talks, however the move is already fading a bit. We are currently trading +2-3bps wider vs, Friday‘s close.

 

*** Equities ***

📉 The weekly rally in global equities lost steam on Friday ahead of weekend peace talks on the Iran conflict. Most indices still closed in the green but gave back most gains during the second half of the day with Trump ramping up threats should negotiations fail and Iran responding accordingly. This morning we are seeing a relatively muted response so far to failed peace talks. Asian shares are down -1% on average. European equity futures are trading -1.5% lower while those in the US are marked less than -1% lower.

 

*** FX ***

💲 The USD opened firm on Friday but gave back initial gains during the second half and into US CPI release. The DXY dropped from 99 intraday highs to levels near 98.55 just to recover slightly into the close. The USD jumped initially this morning but his fading since with DXY sitting just above 99.

 

*** Oil ***

🛢 The oil price dropped on Friday from intraday highs of USD 100/bbl to USD 96 levels for WTI. Following failed peace talks over the weekend initial response was a spike back above USD 105 after reopening. We are trading rangebound since between USD 104 and 105.
