Industry View   In-Line
Stephen W Grambling, James F Koehne, CFA

April 10, 2026
Hotel RevPAR trends have continued to accelerate, pointing to upside to both guidance ranges and consensus estimates. This unfolding cyclical rebound in US should more than offset Middle East impacts. Raise estimates / PT. RHP to OW, ~12% tot. return.
Key takeaways
  • Lodging c-corps/REITs have seeing a rebound in demand post the end of the federal govt shutdown, particularly in luxury/upscale and select urban markets.
  • All management teams exercised caution in FY26 guides, likely incorporating potential for geopolitical uncertainty, but trends pt to upside if 2-yr trends hold.
  • Timeshare demand and provisions were also encouraging, but other KPIs like VPG, tour growth, and net owners were mixed, keeping us selective based on execution.
  • We raise FY26E RevPAR in the US, more than offsetting reductions in the Middle East, though we still factor in slower growth than 2-yr implied & hold FY27 yoy.
  • Upgrade RHP to OW from EW w/ ~12% tot. return (7% to PT, 5% div.) as beats should drive re-rating w/ valuation not reflecting better visibility & ROIC vs. peers

What to do with the stocks & key charts

We are more constructive on the RevPAR backdrop for FY26 given the recent acceleration in trends. However, we largely view the bounce as coming off of depressed, one-time headwinds in 2025 rather than setting a new growth path to extrapolate into FY27. In this environment, we are still avoiding chasing high operating leverage, high financial cyclicals, preferring those names with a combination of longer-term visibility and cyclical upside.

In this vein, we upgrade RHP to OW from EW with ~7% upside to our 12-month PT plus a 5% dividend yield. The main drivers of our upgrade include 1) RevPAR acceleration, particularly RevPAR acceleration in group RevPAR (~70% of RHP mix), 2) a widening gap between RHP's best-in-class total RevPAR and that of full-service hotel REIT peers, (Exhibit 6) and 3) a more compelling valuation set-up after the stock's multiple has been compressed, at a discount vs. history and peers (Exhibit 10). We see upside to consensus estimates from a combination of 1) group demand recovery following last year's disruption from policy-related uncertainty; 2) ramping renovations that are poised to be completed by mid-2026; and 3) continued secular growth in live entertainment in the OEG segment. These factors put us ~2% above consensus FY27 EBITDA and we see valuation moving from ~10.4x 2027 EV/EBITDA to ~11.7x (vs. historical ~12x NTM multiple). As a bonus, we could see optionality if the company can further monetize its OEG segment based on the recent rally in the closest comps, which now trade at ~14.8x 2027e EV/EBITDA (vs. historical ~14.x NTM multiple ï¿½ MSGE and LYV, both covered by Cameron Mansson-Perrone).

Beyond RHP, we stay OW most c-corps beyond RevPAR upside for visibility in room growth, AI optionality, and ancillary upside. Hyatt now screens as having the best risk-adjusted return in our lodging coverage and we expect strength in core RevPAR to outweigh uncertainty in the distribution segment from Mexico/Caribbean as well as impact from Middle East. We also stay OW TNL in timeshare given an upgraded owner base and potential for accelerating fundamentals from new brand expansion. The RAR for HGV has once again improved after underperformance post our downgrade, but we look for greater visibility on competitive impact across the group as VAC (UW) effectuates changes. We remain UW CHH / PEB despite upside to RevPAR as we see both facing idiosyncratic pressures longer-term from competitive threats and need for incremental investment, respectively.
