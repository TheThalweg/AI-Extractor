Investors are viewing the recent US-Iran ceasefire as the first step to restoring the prior 2026 bullish narrative of 1) Trump market support before the November mid-terms, 2) German Fiscal Stimulus, 3) US Fiscal Stimulus & 4) AI capex. The hope here is that the bullish tailwinds from these drivers can outweigh the impact of expensive valuations to support broader risk. For tactical risk-taking over the next 2-3 weeks, spreads could consolidate around current levels. Our geopolitical strategist believes there is a high probability of a longer-term solution to the US-Iran conflict. Despite the lack of a US/Iran deal this weekend, a more positive resolution to Middle East tensions over the next couple of weeks without more significant military escalation (i.e. US "boots on the ground") is still probable. In addition, strong April seasonals and limited cash market supply amidst earnings blackouts will certainly help the technical story.

But the trajectory thereafter should still be moderately wider. The potential of the dual-pronged shock of higher rates and energy costs putting downward pressure on global growth and corporate profit margins notwithstanding, the trajectory of private credit is miles different from what most had assumed in their 2026 Outlooks. And that should feed through to tighter US financial conditions.

As we will show in this piece, private credit fund redemption requests via private BDCs are piling up (Fig 1), with only the gating of outflows preventing more material pressure on the asset class. But redemption gating is highly unlikely to be a cure-all for two reasons. First, if new inflows slow, private credit demand could shift decisively into net outflow territory, even if these managers continue to gate, and that will lead to leveraged loan sales and/or draws on unutilized bank revolvers in short order. But second and more importantly, the problem here is not just liquidity. Public markets are telling us that fundamentals are worsening, notably in the critical software sector, as AI disruption worries swirl. Initial private credit software loan to value ratios may have been 40%, but it is equally true that software multiples have deflated by 44% since 2023 & 2024. And this shock higher in implied software LTVs isn't being priced by private credit. And we estimate that Q4'25 private credit software distress ratios (share of loans with px < 80) were around 1% or less, vs. 9.6% & 24.4% for traded Lev Loan software names in Q4ï¿½25 & Q1ï¿½26 respectively. Redemption gating isn't likely to stop the pressure on private credit, until private credit managers take more meaningful & more numerous software loan markdowns in the quarters ahead.

BDC liquidity pressure won't abate, until we see more private credit software loan write-downs in the quarters ahead.
